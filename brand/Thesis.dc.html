The thesis · delta-one hedge

Going long the H100 perp hedges your bill.

Your compute cost is GPU-hours times the rental rate. The H100 perpetual tracks that rate — so a long position pays you exactly when the rate rises, offsetting the part of your bill that just went up.

03 — Why it's correct

The offset, proven.

If the rate moves from P to P′, your extra compute cost on Q hours is Q × (P′ − P). The long perp of size S gains S × (P′ − P). With S = h × Q, the perp offsets exactly h of the cost change.

A defensible, delta-one hedge — no cleverness required.
