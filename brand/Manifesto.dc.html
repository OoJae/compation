Manifesto

Compute is the new commodity.

It should trade like one.

A short argument for why the price of intelligence deserves a market — and an agent to actually use it.

01 — THE COST

Every company building AI has the same biggest number on its P&L: GPU compute. It is the cost of training, the cost of serving, the cost of every token. And it moves — H100 rental rates swing with demand spikes and supply crunches, sometimes doubling a budget overnight.

Oil has a hedge. Wheat has a hedge. Interest rates have a hedge. Compute — the input to everything — had none.

02 — THE MARKET

That changed. Injective put the first on-chain NVIDIA H100 rental-rate market live, priced hourly by an oracle. For the first time, the cost of compute is something you can take a position on — long or short, on-chain, settled in seconds.

03 — THE GAP

But a perpetual futures market is not a product a founder can use. Sizing, margin, liquidation, funding — it is a trader's instrument, written in a trader's language. The market existed. The usability did not.

A market is not a tool until someone can actually hold it.

04 — THE AGENT

Compation is the tool. You say your burn in plain language; it reasons about strategy, sizes the hedge in deterministic code, and opens a real position on-chain — with a tx hash you can click. The trader's instrument becomes a single sentence.

05 — THE LOOP

And then it does something stranger. Compation pays for its own compute — settling a micropayment on-chain for the data and inference it used. An agent that spends money on compute, and hedges the price of the very thing it spends on. The loop closes on itself.

Compute + Computation — and a little compassion for every founder watching their GPU bill.
